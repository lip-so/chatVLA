📊 DataBench Metrics Guide

a Action Consistency
Evaluates how well the dataset maintains consistency between visual content, text descriptions, actions, and observations across time.
What it measures:
  • Visual-Text Alignment: How well videos match task descriptions (using CLIP similarity)
  • Action-Observation Consistency: Correlation between commanded actions and observed state changes
  • Cross-Episode Consistency: Consistency of actions across episodes with the same task
  • Temporal Consistency: Stability of visual-text alignment over time within episodes
Why it matters: Consistent datasets lead to more reliable robot learning and better generalization.
v Visual Diversity
Measures the variety and richness of visual scenes, environments, and conditions in the dataset.
What it measures:
  • Pairwise Distances: Average visual similarity between different video frames
  • Clustering Analysis: How well visual content separates into distinct groups
  • Entropy Measures: Information-theoretic diversity of visual features
Why it matters: Diverse visual data helps robots adapt to different environments and lighting conditions.
h High-Fidelity Vision
Assesses the technical quality and setup of visual data collection for optimal robot learning.
What it measures:
  • Multiple Views: Number and quality of camera angles (multi-view bonus)
  • Resolution & Framerate: Video technical specifications (HD vs 4K, fps)
  • Environment Quality: Lighting, blur detection, object visibility
  • Prompt Quality: Clarity and specificity of task descriptions
Why it matters: High-quality visual data is essential for precise manipulation and scene understanding.
t Trajectory Quality
Evaluates the temporal structure, synchronization, and completeness of robot trajectory data.
What it measures:
  • Synchronization: Time alignment between different data streams (≤5ms = excellent)
  • Frequency Analysis: Data collection rate (≥30Hz = excellent, ≥10Hz = good)
  • Data Completeness: Percentage of non-null values across the trajectory
  • Temporal Consistency: Smooth progression of states over time
Why it matters: Well-synchronized, high-frequency data enables precise motion learning and control.
c Dataset Coverage
Measures the scale, diversity, and comprehensive coverage of tasks, scenarios, and edge cases.
What it measures:
  • Scale Analysis: Total number of trajectories (50k+ = excellent, 1k+ = good)
  • Task Diversity: Variety of different manipulation tasks (10+ = excellent)
  • Visual Variety: Range of objects, backgrounds, and conditions
  • Failure Analysis: Inclusion of failed attempts and edge cases
Why it matters: Comprehensive coverage ensures robust learning across diverse real-world scenarios.
r Robot Action Quality
Analyzes the quality, smoothness, and physical feasibility of robot actions and commands.
What it measures:
  • Action Smoothness: Continuity and jerk minimization in robot motions
  • Joint Limits: Compliance with physical constraints of the robot
  • Velocity Profiles: Realistic acceleration and speed patterns
  • Gripper Control: Appropriate opening/closing patterns and timing
Why it matters: High-quality actions lead to safer, more efficient robot behavior and better imitation learning.

🎯 Score Interpretation Guide

0.8 - 1.0: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Excellent - Professional quality, ready for deployment
0.6 - 0.8: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Good - High quality with minor improvements needed
0.4 - 0.6: ⭐⭐⭐ Moderate - Acceptable quality, some issues to address
0.2 - 0.4: ⭐⭐ Poor - Significant improvements needed
0.0 - 0.2: ⭐ Very Poor - Major issues, requires substantial work
